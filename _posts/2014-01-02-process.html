Bringing Design to Your Organization

In this section, we outline key steps in a design process. You can apply them to designing new web services, mobile applications, as well as in person service interactions.

User Research

User research helps you channel the needs, desires, and motivations of the people who use what you create.

Learn More

Ideation

Quickly generate, sort, and prioritize ideas to create solutions.

Learn More

Concept Development

Shape your best ideas into more complete concepts including what you could prototype to test out the concept.

Learn More

Experience Maps

Chart the experience moments including steps in a process and the emotions and feelings, such as delight or satisfaction, as well as the decisions that a user must make.

Learn More

Content Strategy

Work with writers and other content contributors to create a strategy for how information is incorporated into your project including its sources, display, and tone.

Learn More

Information Architecture

Catalog and categorize the information and content of what you’re creating based on how the user will understand it.

Learn More

Wireframes

Lay out where content and elements go on the interface, considering their hierarchy and modes of engagement.

Learn More

Visual Design

Create visual styles for the elements in the wireframes.

Learn More

Prototype

Find the minimal part of the idea that can test to see if it’s worth building on a larger scale..

Learn More

Test

Take your prototype out into the world and test it with real users.

Learn More

Iterate

Use what you learned through testing to continually revise and build upon your idea.

Learn More
