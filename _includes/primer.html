What is Design?

Private companies like Apple and public agencies like the United Kingdom’s Government Digital Service have demonstrated the power of design. Whether you are a small department or a whole city government, your services require design to be effective and efficient in our fast-changing world.

What is ‘design’ though? It isn't just your brand. It’s more than color and typography. Design isn’t how your website looks, design is how it works for visiting citizens. At it's core, Design is a set of values that help us solve problems. As a result, design a tool to help you deliver services that meet the ever evolving needs of your constituents.

This primer will help you understand design and provide resources to help you find the design talent government needs.

Let's start with what many of us know. You’re probably familiar with the Classic Design Disciplines. We have good mental images for many types of design such as graphic design, industrial design, and architecture. After all, humans have been practicing these disciplines for thousands of years.

With each new wave of technology, new design disciplines have bloomed. While their antecedents are often millennia old as well, we’ve only defined these practices as distinct crafts with the rise of computers and the Internet. In our modern culture, these design disciplines are key to transforming public services:

Design is in everything we make, but it’s also between those things. It’s a mix of craft, science, storytelling, propaganda, and philosophy.

Erik Adigard

User Experience

Consideration of people’s experience using a system, including their emotional responses, such as delight or satisfaction, as well as the decisions that a user must make. User experience design is built on learnings from psychology, sociology, anthropology and cognitive science.

User Interface

The means by which people interact with a digital or physical product. We use these all the time, but we don’t call them user interfaces, we call them Google Search or the Facebook like button, or Windows. Good user interface design emphasizes goals and accomplishing tasks, and asks no more attention from people than is required.

Architecture information and data are organized and how you want your user to understand the information you’re trying to convey. It helps catalog and categorize the information, in order to the most relevant information findable and usable.

What unites the modern design disciplines?

They focus on the User

The real revelation with these newer forms of design is that they put the user at the center. Designers are creating with their users in mind and working them for feedback and testing, rather than designing for purely aesthetic intent.

They’re about how things work.

Our modern disciplines of design are about how things work for people. Designers are creating systems.

They’ve evolved with technology.

Tech has evolved a lot very quickly, changing the way we interact with our world. There are a lot of different devices now, with a lot of different variables from operating systems to screen sizes. This shouldn’t be thought of as a challenge, but rather an opportunity to meet our users where they are and engage them in new ways.

Most people make the mistake of thinking design is what it looks like. People think it’s this veneer – that the designers are handed this box and told, ‘Make it look good!’ That’s not what we think design is. It’s not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works.

Steve Jobs

What makes good design?

There are many qualities that make for good design, but Dieter Rams, a famous designer, has developed 10 principles that can help guide design evaluation.

Good design is innovative

Good design makes a product useful

Good design is aesthetic

Good design makes a product understandable

Good design is honest

Good design is long-lasting

Good design is thorough down to the last detail

Good design is environmentally friendly

Good design is unobtrusive

Good design is as little design as possible

Why Design Matters in Government

We believe that good design can make public services more effective and efficient, and more responsive to citizens needs. Perhaps most importantly, we believe that when public services meet citizens needs, it builds trust between citizens and government.

Practitioners around the world are reshaping government services in their community. Whether high-tech and low-tech, each of these efforts place the citizen’s needs at the center of their thinking. They are committed to understanding citizen needs through a deliberate empathy. They embrace humility, continuous learning, and iterative improvement. They are bringing Design to government.

There are many avenues to get started in bringing design to your organization. They include everything from adopting a design process, to building effective teams, to including design in contracting.
